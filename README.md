# Metal Moon

A dark science-fantasy tabletop RPG campaign set on TerraLuna, a metal asteroid moon where colonial survivors navigate political conspiracy, environmental hazards, and buried truths.

## Overview

TerraLuna is a metal moon in orbit around the gas giant Typhon. Seventy years ago, human colonists established settlements on this hostile world, mining precious metals and struggling against gravity anomalies, extreme cold, and metallic life forms. But beneath the surface survival story lies a darker truth: **The Lottery**, a conspiracy that shaped the colony's founding and continues to haunt its present.

## The World

**TerraLuna** - A metallic asteroid moon with:
- Thin, hostile atmosphere (breathable but harsh outside settlements — cold, ionized, metallic)
- Gravity anomalies from orbital decay
- Temperatures averaging -40°C to -60°C
- Exposed meteoritic iron with Widmanstätten patterns
- Pallasite olivine crystals (amber-green, glowing when backlit)
- Metallic dust deserts covering the surface
- Rusties: metal-dissolving insects, the moon's only complex native life

**New-Rhode** - Primary city-state, home to 50,000+ colonists organized into guilds:
- Desk Admins (bureaucrats)
- Deployed Admins (law enforcement)
- Miners (resource extraction)
- GraviMancers (gravity manipulation specialists)
- TrailBlazers (desert nomads, exiled faction)

## The Central Mystery: The Lottery

Seventy years ago, during a resource crisis, the colony's founders implemented "The Choice" policy:
- Families with surnames beginning with letters S-Z were classified as "betrayers"
- They were given a choice: exile to the deadly desert or have their children frozen in cryo-sleep
- Forty-seven children were frozen, supposedly sent to sleep for 1-2 years until resources improved
- Instead, they slept for 40 years, waking as adults with no memory of parents
- Today, they're second-class citizens, tattooed with alphanumeric designations, struggling against prejudice

**The conspiracy**: The founders lied. The Lottery wasn't about betrayal - it was systematic oppression. The frozen children are descendants of innocent families, and the truth has been buried for seven decades.

## The Campaign

The campaign begins with an assassination attempt on Governor Liliane FoxKilo, the first "alphabetic-surnamed" citizen to hold political power. The shooter, Alastair Redhand, is a former Deployed Admin turned truth-seeker who believes Liliane is complicit in covering up The Lottery conspiracy.

**The twist**: Liliane is innocent. She's one of the frozen children, unaware that her birth father Marcus Kaine was the architect of The Choice policy. She's been preaching forgiveness and redemption without knowing she's inadvertently covering up her own father's crimes.

Over ten sessions, player characters uncover:
- Cryo facility records showing coordinated mass freezing
- Founder documents revealing The Choice policy
- TrailBlazer oral histories from exiled families
- Marcus Kaine's hidden vault and suicide confession
- The truth about "alphabetic-surnamed" citizens' parentage

The campaign culminates in a choice: reveal the truth and destabilize society, or maintain the lie to preserve order.

## Visual Style: Sgraffito Industrial Expressionism

The campaign's signature aesthetic combines:
- **Sgraffito technique**: Thick paint layers scraped to reveal metallic textures
- **Industrial palette**: Grays, deep shadows, aggressive accent colors
- **High contrast**: Stark lighting with no atmospheric softening
- **Strong silhouettes**: Characters recognizable from outline
- **Intentional texture**: Every mark tells TerraLuna's harsh story

Character-specific accent colors and location-specific palettes create visual distinction while maintaining cohesive style.
