# DUST DESERT
## TerraLuna's Metallic Wasteland

**Location Type**: Hostile wilderness, travel hazard, TrailBlazer territory
**Region**: Surrounds New-Rhode and other city-states
**Significance**: Primary barrier between cities, survival challenge, home to TrailBlazers

---

## PHYSICAL DESCRIPTION

**Terrain**:
- **Surface**: Fine metallic dust (pulverized meteor debris) covering hard metal substrate
- **Color**: Gray-silver with rust-red patches (iron oxide concentrations)
- **Topography**: Relatively flat with frequent crater impacts creating irregular elevation changes
- **Visibility**: Varies wildly—100+ ft in clear conditions, 10 ft during dust storms
- **Horizon**: Black sky meets silver ground in knife-edge line (no atmosphere diffusion)

**Key Features**:
- **Crater Fields**: Impact sites from meteor debris belt, ranging from 10 ft to 1000 ft diameter
- **Metal Ridges**: Upthrust substrate where tectonic shifts exposed solid metal
- **Dust Dunes**: Wind-sculpted formations (15-30 ft tall) that shift during dust storms
- **Founder Ruins**: Scattered equipment from early colonization (70+ years of exposure)
- **Rustie Nests**: Metallic mounds where insect colonies burrow

**Sky**:
- Black void with visible stars (no atmosphere to scatter light)
- TerraLuna's parent gas giant looms large on horizon (variable position)
- Meteor streaks common (high-activity debris belt)
- No clouds, no weather patterns (dust is ground-level only)

**Climate**:
- **Temperature**: -40°C average, colder at night (-60°C)
- **Wind**: Constant moderate breeze (10-20 mph), violent during storms (50+ mph)
- **Gravity**: Unstable (post-event fluctuations common in wasteland)
- **Radiation**: Moderate (no magnetic field protection, short exposure tolerable)

---

## SURVIVAL CHALLENGES

**Environmental Hazards**:

**1. Cold Exposure**
- **Unprotected**: CON save DC 12 every hour, 1d6 cold damage on failure
- **Proper Gear**: No saves required for up to 12 hours
- **Frostbite**: Three failed saves = Exhaustion level 1

**2. Dust Storms**
- **Frequency**: 1-in-6 chance per 4-hour travel period
- **Effects**: Visibility reduced to 10 ft, 1d6 slashing damage per hour from abrasive particles
- **Protection**: Breathers + sealed clothing prevent damage
- **Navigation**: Survival checks at disadvantage

**3. Gravity Fluctuations**
- **Frequency**: Random (DM discretion, 1d10 hours between events)
- **Effects**: DEX save DC 13 or thrown 1d20 ft in random direction, 1d6 bludgeoning damage
- **Duration**: 1d6 rounds
- **GraviMancer Advantage**: GraviMancer PCs have advantage on saves

**4. Rustie Encounters**
- **Frequency**: Travel through metal-rich areas = 1-in-4 chance per 4 hours
- **Warning Signs**: Metallic clicking, disturbed dust patterns, heat signatures
- **Detection**: Survival DC 12 or TrailBlazer auto-detects

**5. Disorientation**
- **Problem**: No landmarks, featureless terrain, magnetic interference
- **Navigation**: Survival DC 13 every 4 hours or become lost
- **Lost**: Wander in circle, no progress toward destination
- **TrailBlazer**: Auto-succeeds on navigation (profession feature)

**6. Resource Depletion**
- **Water**: 2 units per day (cold environment = dehydration slower)
- **Food**: 1 ration per day
- **Breather Filters**: 1 per dust storm encounter (or 24 hours continuous)
- **Heat Sources**: Heat-tabs burn 4 hours each (required for rest periods)

## NAVIGATION & TRAVEL

**Travel Rates**:
- **Normal Terrain**: 24 miles per day (careful pace)
- **Crater Fields**: 18 miles per day (difficult terrain)
- **During Dust Storm**: 6 miles per day (or camp until it passes)
- **With TrailBlazer Guide**: +25% to all rates

**Distance Markers** (from New-Rhode):
- **TrailBlazer Camp**: 32 miles (12 hours travel)
- **The Scar**: 50 miles (20 hours travel)
- **Next Nearest City-State** (Tantalos): 200+ miles (8+ days)

**Landmark Features** (for navigation):
- **Crater 7-Alpha**: Large impact site, visible from 10 miles
- **Iron Ridge**: Rust-red metal outcropping, runs north-south for 15 miles
- **Founder's Bones**: Crashed founder-era shuttle, stripped to frame
- **TrailBlazer Cairns**: Rock stacks marking safe routes (hidden to outsiders)

## FLORA & FAUNA

**Rusties** (Dominant Species):
- Metal-dissolving insects, hard carapace (immune to cold)
- Nest in metal-rich areas
- Aggressive if nest threatened
- Active during low-gravity periods (easier movement)
- See stat block [Session 1 Main Document]

**Trace Life**:
- **Subsurface Microbes**: Chemosynthetic organisms in underground ice
- **Frost Lichens**: Rare, grow on founder ruins (source of TrailBlazer medicine)
- **Nothing Else**: Surface is sterile; TerraLuna has no native complex life

**Adapted Life** (TrailBlazers):
- Humans who've survived multiple generations in wasteland
- Knowledge passed down (survival techniques, safe routes, Rustie behavior)
- Scattered camps and semi-permanent settlements

## TACTICAL INFORMATION

**Cover & Concealment**:
- **Dust Dunes**: Half cover, unstable (Athletics DC 10 to climb)
- **Crater Rims**: Full cover if lying prone, elevation advantage
- **Metal Ridges**: Full cover, climbable (Athletics DC 12)
- **Founder Ruins**: Variable cover, may collapse (unsafe)

**Line of Sight**:
- Clear conditions: Perception limited by curvature (5-mile horizon)
- Dust clouds: Heavily obscured beyond 10 ft
- Darkness: TerraLuna has minimal ambient light (dark vision advantaged)

**Movement**:
- Dust surface: Difficult terrain for running (Athletics DC 10 or speed halved)
- Metal substrate: Slippery when exposed (Acrobatics DC 12)
- Low-gravity zones: Jump distances doubled

**Ambush Opportunities**:
- Crater depressions (hide below sightline, Stealth advantage)
- Dust storms (invisible beyond 10 ft)
- Rustie nest disguise (bury in dust near metallic mounds)

## DM NOTES

**Running Desert Travel**:
- **Don't Skip It**: Make travel feel dangerous and meaningful
- **Skill Challenges**: Use group checks for navigation/survival
- **Random Encounters**: 1-2 per crossing (balance challenge/pacing)
- **Resource Tracking**: Emphasize scarcity (rations, water, heat-tabs)
- **Weather**: Dust storm adds tension and forces decisions

**Atmosphere Goals**:
- TerraLuna is hostile (constant low-level threat)
- Party is vulnerable (no backup, no safety)
- Beauty in harshness (describe alien vistas)
- Scale matters (they're insects crossing infinite plain)

**Pacing**:
- **Act 1 to Act 2 Transition**: Desert crossing should take 30-45 minutes real-time
- **Break Points**: TrailBlazer camp is natural rest/social encounter
- **Climax Setup**: Arriving at Scar should feel like reaching final dungeon

**Difficulty Scaling**:
- **Easy**: Clear weather, no encounters, straight navigation
- **Medium**: One dust storm or Rustie fight, normal navigation
- **Hard**: Multiple encounters, gravity event mid-travel, lost navigation

## DESCRIPTIVE READ-ALOUDS

**First Impression**:
> "The airlock opens. Beyond lies TerraLuna's true face—a silver-dust wasteland stretching to black infinity. The cold hits immediately, stealing breath. Wind carries metallic particles that sting exposed skin. Above, the gas giant Typhon looms impossibly large, swirling storms visible in its atmosphere. No sun. No clouds. No life. Just metal, dust, and void. New-Rhode's lights fade behind you. You are alone."

**Dust Storm**:
> "The horizon darkens. Not black—metallic gray, a wall of dust advancing at terrifying speed. Wind shifts, screaming across the plain. Then it hits. Visibility drops to arm's length. Dust particles scour exposed surfaces. Your breathers filter frantically. The world becomes a whirling chaos of gray, wind, and cold. This is how TerraLuna kills."

**Gravity Event** (mid-travel):
> "It starts with vertigo. Your inner ear screams wrong. Then you feel it—gravity pulling lateral, not down. Dust lifts in curtains, swirling in impossible directions. You stagger, fighting to stay grounded. The TrailBlazer drops flat, magnetic clamps engaging. 'HOLD ON!' Too late. The world lurches. You're pulled sideways, twenty feet, slamming into a crater rim. And just as suddenly: normal. Gravity returns. Dust settles. Your heart hammers. Welcome to TerraLuna."

**Approaching TrailBlazer Camp**:
> "The canyon opens like a wound in the metal plain—a hidden scar invisible until you're standing at its edge. Below, sheltered from wind, you see them: makeshift structures, a real wood fire (impossibly precious), and figures watching you. One steps forward. Old, weathered, sharp-eyed. 'City-folk,' she says. Not a question. An assessment. 'You're hunting Alastair. We gave him water. We'll give you the same. Whether we give you more... that depends.'"
