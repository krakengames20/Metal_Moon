# Crossing the Dust Desert

Cold, thin air. Day and night. Nights are colder and darker - usually stop and camp.  Dusty and windy.  Pale grey rocks around New Rhodes. Fine, metallic sand when you get further out.

Vehicles ,(mules) are like wagons riding on big, puffy wheels. Rock and roll a lot over the rough terrain. Lumbers. Electric, with solar top up.  Tall, fibre glass mask with a light and some glitter, reflective foil on top.  Batteries good for a days travel, then need to camp for a second day with panels out to recharge. Can press on into night for 2 hours or so - but then the vehicle shuts down until recharged. Katie, Sally, Lilly, Abby. Garvin and Cass in Katie, taking the lead. Tanya riding with Marion.  The other 3 are AI piloted, on a follow me program.  They can all talk on the CB channel.

Marion's mule (Barnabus) has an on board nuke, so unlimited power, Max speed is still 10kph or so. She'll want to get some more fuel at the hot rocks. 

Camp is usually forming a circle with tents in the middle. They can sleep in the vehicles if they must.

Following hazerds for travelling, we need to pick 2-3 for each chapter where travel occurs.

## The Boiling Sea

Tracks lead towards a column of smoke. Crossing a low Ridge they see them descending towards a shallow steaming , sea.  Warm air billows around them. Garvin calls a halt 'We're chasing a fool.' 'Why?' 'The Boiling Sea. Fifteen cm deep and 15 km wide. There's a massive natural fission reactor under it. Idiot'll get a lethal dose if he isn't careful.' 'So we can't go in after him?' 'Nah. Gotta go around and pick up his tracks where he came out.' Garvin looks up at the sky. 'We'll look for somewhere to camp, only a few hours daylight left. Doubt we'll get around it by nightfall.' Marion: 'Yeah, good plan. I'll go in and get some samples tomorrow.' 'Still trying to get yourself killed?' 'Nah, I got some suits.' 'Fair enough, your life.'

Come of hours later they set up camp, forming the wagons into a circle, plugging them together and deploying large solar charging arrays that would start recharging the batteries in the morning. Dinner in camp, some chatter around the fire then turning in.

Next day is bright and sunny. Garvin checks the charge - higher than expected and now charging from the solar arrays. He tells them they'll be charged enough to move on tomorrow. 

Marion says she's going into the sea to get some rocks. Asks Cass and Tanya if they want to join her; she's got some spare suits. Trick in, get some yellow rocks and mud, put it in a small wagon, back to the convoy.

Head off next morning, find Alistars tracks, start following them again.

## Rusty River.

Garvin calls a halt. There's a strange stream flowing across the path. 'Damn, new one.' 'New what?' 'Rusty river.' 'Eh?' 'Rusties are native insects; they eat metal. Sometimes they migrate, like this. Don't go in there.' he chucks a small piece of scrap in. The rivers smooth flow changes, swirling and bubbling. When it calms the scrap is gone. 'Marion, we need a bridge!'

## Sandstorm

The skies grow dark and cold. Garvin and Marion decide whether to camp or run. No where to run to, so they camp.  Wagons in a circle, cables between them, solar collectors down. Storm swirls and passes overhead. Lots of shrieking wind.

The storm passes and they start digging mules out.  Alistairs tracks are gone - but a reflective balloon can be seen in the distance. Garvin takes his time packing up, but he can't refuses Cass's request to investigate it.  In late afternoon, the convoy rolls up to the outskirts of the Trail Blazers camp.

## Twister

Big, tornado like wind storm, pullng sand up and throwing it up into the air, while stripping the area under it.  Mules hunker down in a circle, chained together, solar panels stashed, and hope they are heavy enough not to become airborune.  Lots of shaking and shuddering, then resetting the convoy, recharging and climbing out of the gully. 
Tracks damaged, but effect is fairly localized (2-3km).

## Rainstorm

Acid rain starts falling.  Garvin stops the mules on the nearest high ground, then every one out to stow the solar panels and pull raincovers out the top of each of the mules. 
Sit the rainstorm out in the cold and dark, watch the aacid water rise below them. rain stops overnight, water drains away. Uncover the Mules and spend a day charging before moving on.
Damages tracks within the rain area (5km).
