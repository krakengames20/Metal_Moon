# THE SCAR
## Massive Crater Network and Founder Ruins

**Location Type**: Unstable crater network, founder archaeological site, Session 1 climax location
**Region**: Deep desert, 50 miles southwest of New-Rhode
**Significance**: Alastair's destination, founder cache location, major revelation site

---

## PHYSICAL DESCRIPTION

**Overall Structure**:
- **Formation**: Network of interconnected craters from cascading meteor impacts (70+ years ago)
- **Size**: 2-mile diameter main crater with 15-20 smaller subsidiary craters
- **Depth**: Ranges from 50 ft (shallow craters) to 300 ft (main crater center)
- **Shape**: Irregular, overlapping circles creating labyrinthine terrain

**Terrain Features**:
- **Crater Rims**: Sharp-edged ridges (unstable, prone to collapse)
- **Impact Floors**: Compacted metal substrate (stable but exposed)
- **Talus Slopes**: Loose debris fields between levels (difficult terrain, avalanche risk)
- **Central Depression**: Deepest point, contains founder memorial and ruins
- **Metal Outcroppings**: Exposed substrate forming natural pillars and arches

**Founder Ruins**:
- **Landing Site Remains**: Broken framework from original colony ships (70+ years weathering)
- **Supply Caches**: Hidden storage bunkers (some intact, most Rustie-damaged)
- **Memorial Plaque**: Founder-era inscription at center ("For those who made The Choice")
- **Equipment Wreckage**: Scattered machinery, vehicles, construction equipment
- **Founder Cache** (hidden): Intact bunker requiring puzzle solution to access

---

## GRAVITY ANOMALIES

**Unique Characteristic**: Most Gravitationally Unstable Region on TerraLuna

**Causes**:
- Orbital decay effects strongest here (geographical bad luck)
- Dense metal concentration creates local gravitational complications
- Cascading crater structure channels gravitational forces unpredictably

**Effects**:

**Gravity Event Frequency**: Roll 1d6 every 10 minutes:
- **1-2**: Lateral Pull (sideways gravity shift)
- **3**: Inversion (gravity weakens significantly)
- **4**: Crush (gravity intensifies)
- **5**: Vortex (swirling pull toward center point)
- **6**: Stillness (brief respite, 1d10 minutes of stable gravity)

**Mechanical Effects**:
- **Lateral Pull**: DEX save DC 13 or slide 10 ft east/west
- **Inversion**: Jump distances tripled, falling damage halved (1 minute duration)
- **Crush**: Speed halved, STR save DC 13 or knocked prone (1 minute duration)
- **Vortex**: STR save DC 14 or pulled 15 ft toward crater center
- **Stillness**: No effects (safe travel window)

**GraviMancer Advantage**:
- GraviMancer PCs have advantage on all saves
- Can stabilize one ally within 10 ft as reaction
- Can sense incoming gravity shifts (WIS check DC 12, 1 round warning)

## TACTICAL INFORMATION

**Combat Terrain**:
- **Elevation**: Crater rims provide high ground (+2 to ranged attacks downward)
- **Cover**: Abundant (ruins, outcroppings, crater walls)
- **Movement**: Difficult terrain (loose debris, unstable slopes)
- **Hazards**: Gravity anomalies mid-combat, collapsing terrain
- **Line of Sight**: Broken (crater structure creates many blind spots)

**Navigating the Scar**:
- **Easy Route** (along rim): Safe but exposed, 30 minutes to center
- **Direct Route** (through craters): Fast (15 minutes) but difficult terrain and gravity hazards
- **Ruins Route**: Most cover but navigation complex (Survival DC 14 or get lost)

**Collapse Risks**:
- **Crater Rims**: Athletics check DC 12 when moving along edge, fall 2d6x10 ft on failure
- **Talus Slopes**: Disturbing loose debris may trigger avalanche (DEX save DC 13, 3d6 bludgeoning)
- **Ruins**: Structural instability (DM discretion, usually combat triggers collapses)

**Ambush Opportunities**:
- Alastair knows this terrain (scouted previously)
- Can trigger gravity events or avalanches tactically
- Multiple firing positions from crater rims
- Center depression is natural kill zone (surrounded by high ground)

## FOUNDER CACHE

**Location**: Hidden beneath memorial plaque in central depression

**Entrance**: Sealed airlock (70+ years undiscovered)

**Puzzle: Airlock Activation** (See Session 1 Main Document):
- Three pressure plates must be activated simultaneously
- Requires teamwork and guild-specific skills
- Failure consequences: Gravity anomaly triggers, 2d6 bludgeoning damage

**Interior**:
- **Size**: 20 ft x 30 ft storage room
- **Condition**: Preserved (sealed environment, no Rustie damage)
- **Contents**:
  - Founder survival equipment (200 credits value)
  - Energy cells (restore GraviMancer charges)
  - Emergency medical kit (3 uses, heals 2d4+2 HP)
  - **MAJOR DISCOVERY**: Cryo Pod Activation Log (partial, corrupted) [See Session 1 Main Doc]

**Log Significance**:
Shows all 47 "betrayer children" frozen on same day ("Day of Resolution"), proving coordinated event rather than gradual departure. Marcus Kaine's authorization signature. First solid evidence of Lottery conspiracy.

## MEMORIAL PLAQUE

**Location**: Center of deepest crater
**Material**: Rhodium plate (tarnished but legible)
**Inscription**:

```
FOR THOSE WHO MADE THE CHOICE
In Memory of the Founders
Who Sacrificed for TerraLuna's Survival
Their Burden We Bear
Their Courage We Honor
[Date: 70 Years Ago]
```

**Significance**:
- "The Choice" direct reference (hidden in plain sight)
- Alastair brought party here to show them this
- Ambiguous language: sacrifice, burden, choice—all loaded with conspiracy context
- Founder-era memorial suggests "The Choice" was official, documented policy

**Alastair's Position**:
When party arrives, he's kneeling before plaque, crossbow set aside. He's been waiting. This is his evidence—the beginning of their education.

## HISTORICAL SIGNIFICANCE

**70+ Years Ago** (Founding Era):
- One of original colony landing sites
- Supply caches established here for expansion
- Central staging area before cities built
- Where Marcus Kaine and other founders debated The Choice policy
- Memorial erected after Night of Chains (commemorating "those who chose")

**Hidden History**:
- Mass departure point (fleeing families left from here toward deep desert)
- Some executions occurred here (far from city, hidden)
- Founder cache sealed with evidence (insurance policy? guilty conscience?)
- Memorial is both tribute and tombstone—depends on perspective

**Current State**:
- Largely forgotten by city-dwellers (too remote, dangerous)
- TrailBlazers know of it but avoid (gravity instability, Rustie activity)
- Occasionally visited by historical researchers (rarely—too dangerous)
- Alastair researched and located as part of investigation

---

## ENVIRONMENTAL HAZARDS

**1. Gravity Instability** (primary hazard):
- Constant threat (see Gravity Anomalies section)
- Makes extended stay dangerous
- Combat becomes chaotic and unpredictable

**2. Structural Collapse**:
- Crater rims unstable (age and gravity stress)
- Ruins may collapse if disturbed
- Avalanches possible on talus slopes

**3. Rustie Activity**:
- Several nests in outer craters
- Attracted to metal ruins (food source)
- Usually avoid center (gravity instability affects them too)
- Possible random encounter (1-in-6 chance if party lingers 30+ minutes)

**4. Exposure**:
- Fully exposed to elements (no shelter)
- Cold intensifies at night (-50°C in crater depressions)
- Wind funnels through crater network (dust storm danger)
- No water source (must carry all supplies)

**5. Disorientation**:
- Complex terrain makes navigation difficult
- All craters look similar (WIS check DC 12 to maintain direction)
- Gravity shifts compound disorientation
- Easy to get turned around in ruins

## DM NOTES

**Running the Climax**:
- **Approach**: Build tension as party descends into crater network
- **Gravity Events**: Use them to complicate combat or create dramatic moments
- **Alastair Scene**: Don't rush—let confrontation breathe
- **Discovery**: Cache revelation should feel earned (puzzle, then reward)

**Atmosphere Goals**:
- Alien and dangerous (constant threat)
- Historically significant (weight of past)
- Climactic (everything converges here)
- Ambiguous (is Alastair villain or victim?)

**Pacing**:
- **Arrival**: 5 minutes (describe terrain, establish tension)
- **Navigation**: 10 minutes (puzzle/skill challenges)
- **Confrontation**: 20-30 minutes (combat or dialogue with Alastair)
- **Discovery**: 10 minutes (cache access and revelation)
- **Conclusion**: 5 minutes (aftermath, decisions)

**Difficulty Scaling**:
- **Easy**: Stable gravity, Alastair surrenders quickly
- **Medium**: 1-2 gravity events, Alastair negotiates
- **Hard**: Constant gravity chaos, Alastair fights tactically, Rustie interruption

## DESCRIPTIVE READ-ALOUDS

**Approaching the Scar**:
> "The horizon shifts—where flat desert should be, the ground simply... drops. As you approach, the truth emerges: a wound in TerraLuna's surface, a network of craters like splash-damage frozen in metal. The Scar. Even from the rim, you feel it—gravity pulling wrong, your inner ear screaming confusion. Below, amid the twisted maze of impact sites, you see them: founder ruins, skeletal and ancient. And at the center, a glint of rhodium. The memorial. Where Alastair waits."

**Gravity Event** (mid-descent):
> "The shift hits without warning. Your stomach lurches as gravity pivots—suddenly you're not standing on the slope, you're standing on a wall. The world tilts. You scramble for purchase. Loose debris tumbles sideways, clattering upward into the sky. The GraviMancer drops into a crouch, field crackling around them. 'HOLD!' they shout. You cling to whatever you can. And then, as suddenly as it began: normal. Gravity returns. You're on the slope again. But thirty feet from where you started. Welcome to the Scar."

**Memorial Plaque** (discovery):
> "'For Those Who Made The Choice.' The inscription catches starlight, rhodium still gleaming after seventy years. You read it twice. Three times. The words don't change. 'The Choice.' Not betrayal. Not theft. Choice. Sacrifice. Burden. This is a memorial. But to what? To whom? Alastair kneels before it, crossbow discarded. He doesn't turn as you approach. 'Now you see it,' he whispers. 'Now you start to understand.'"

**Founder Cache** (interior):
> "The airlock groans open on hinges that haven't moved in seventy years. Cold air escapes—colder than the desert, sterile and preserved. Inside: rows of storage cases, founder-era equipment, and there—on a terminal screen still glowing faintly on emergency power—a list. FK-382. SN-117. RT-095. Forty-seven names. Forty-seven children. All frozen on the same day. Marcus Kaine's authorization at the bottom. This isn't conjecture anymore. This is evidence."
