# TRAILBLAZER CAMP
## Hidden Canyon Refuge and Desert Settlement

**Location Type**: Semi-permanent settlement, safe haven, information hub
**Region**: Deep desert, 32 miles southwest of New-Rhode
**Significance**: TrailBlazer cultural center, Elder Kora's territory, Session 1 rest/information stop

---

## PHYSICAL DESCRIPTION

**Location**:
- **Formation**: Natural canyon carved by ancient geological activity (pre-colonization)
- **Orientation**: Runs north-south for approximately 2 miles
- **Width**: 300-400 ft at widest point, narrowing to 50 ft in places
- **Depth**: 150 ft below surface level (provides wind shelter and concealment)
- **Access**: Invisible from surface until standing at rim; appears as fissure

**Canyon Features**:
- **Walls**: Exposed metal substrate (rhodium-rich, oxidized to rust-red in places)
- **Floor**: Compacted dust and rock (relatively stable)
- **Ceiling**: Open to sky (no natural cover from above)
- **Caves**: Multiple alcoves and shallow caves in canyon walls (natural shelter)
- **Water Source**: Underground ice seep at canyon base (processed for drinking water—TrailBlazer's most valuable resource)

**Settlement Layout**:
- **Population**: 40-50 TrailBlazers (Elder Kora's extended clan)
- **Structures**: Modular shelters built from salvaged founder equipment, metal sheeting, and scavenged materials
- **Central Fire**: Real wood fire (extremely rare and precious—sourced from ??? [[Awaiting further instructions from creator - where does wood come from on metal moon?]])
- **Lookout Posts**: Two positions on canyon rim (rotating watch, 24/7)
- **Workshop**: Repair station with tools and salvaged parts
- **Medical Tent**: Basic first aid and field medicine
- **Elder's Dwelling**: Largest structure, centrally located (where Kora receives visitors)

**Defensive Features**:
- **Concealment**: Canyon invisible from surface (Perception DC 16 to spot entrance)
- **Choke Points**: Narrow canyon approaches (easily defended)
- **Early Warning**: Lookout posts with signaling mirrors
- **Escape Routes**: Multiple climbing paths to rim (known to TrailBlazers only)
- **Camouflage**: Structures built from desert-toned materials (blend with canyon walls)

## CULTURAL SIGNIFICANCE

**To TrailBlazers**:
- Home and headquarters (Elder Kora's primary settlement)
- Sacred ground (exile ancestors first sheltered here 70 years ago)
- Proving ground (young TrailBlazers come here for training)
- Council site (clan gatherings held quarterly)
- Symbol of independence (city-states can't touch them here)

**Founding Story**:
- Discovered by fleeing exiles during Night of Chains (70 years ago)
- Underground water made survival possible
- Gradually built into permanent settlement
- Now serves as cultural heart of scattered TrailBlazer clans

**Traditions**:
- **Water Sharing**: Offering water to visitors is sacred hospitality (refusing is grave insult)
- **Story Nights**: Elders recite oral histories around central fire
- **Name Beading**: Each bead in a TrailBlazer's hair represents one year survived in desert
- **Exile Remembrance**: Annual ceremony honoring those who fled cities 70 years ago

## TACTICAL INFORMATION

**Defensibility**:
- **Approaches**: Only 3 practical entry points (north canyon mouth, south canyon mouth, western climb path)
- **Sightlines**: Lookouts have clear view for miles (approaching groups spotted hours away)
- **Terrain Advantage**: Defenders have high ground (rim positions), cover (canyon walls), and home terrain knowledge
- **Water Leverage**: Only reliable water source for 50 miles (controls vital resource)

**For Attackers**:
- Canyon walls provide cover for approach
- Narrow spaces negate numerical advantage
- TrailBlazers know terrain intimately (traps, hidden paths)
- Gravity events more dangerous in enclosed space

**For Visitors**:
- Approach openly (sneaking is hostile act)
- Lookouts will challenge (halt and identify)
- Respect protocols (water offering, elder acknowledgment)
- Breaking guest-right = permanent banishment (or worse)

## RESOURCES & SERVICES

**Available to Visitors** (negotiable):
- **Rest**: Safe sleeping area (4-8 hour rest periods)
- **Water**: Purified drinking water (2 credits per unit, free if allied)
- **Food**: Trail rations, dried meat (1 credit per day's supply)
- **Repairs**: Equipment maintenance (20 credits per item, requires 2-4 hours)
- **Medical**: Field medicine, wound treatment (10 credits per treatment)
- **Information**: Route guidance, terrain warnings, desert lore (negotiable, depends on relationship)
- **Rustie Repellent**: Refills (5 credits per use)

**Not Available**:
- Advanced technology repairs (no expertise)
- GraviMancer charge restoration (no equipment)
- Long-term housing (not for outsiders)
- Military support (TrailBlazers don't fight city-folk wars)

**Special Access** (if Elder Kora trusts party):
- Oral history testimony
- Sacred site locations
- Hidden water sources (strategic intelligence)
- TrailBlazer escort services (guides for dangerous areas)

## SOCIAL ENCOUNTER DYNAMICS

**First Contact** (Session 1):
- Lookouts challenge party from rim: "Halt! Identify!"
- Party must explain purpose (hunting Alastair)
- Lookouts report to Kora
- Kora emerges to assess (tests their character)

**Negotiation Structure**:
1. **Initial Distrust**: TrailBlazers assume city-folk are threats
2. **Assessment**: Kora evaluates party's intentions and respect
3. **Conditional Help**: Offers basic aid (rest, water) regardless
4. **Trust Building**: Additional information requires earning respect
5. **Alliance**: If fully trusted, TrailBlazers become valuable allies

**What Kora Wants** (negotiation leverage):
- **Respect**: Acknowledge TrailBlazer culture and independence
- **Information**: What's happening in cities (fair trade)
- **Assurance**: Party won't reveal camp location to authorities
- **Payment**: Credits or trade goods (pragmatic, not greedy)
- **Justice**: If party pursues truth about Lottery, she's more willing to help

**Skill Checks**:
- **Persuasion DC 12**: Gain basic cooperation (water, rest, general info)
- **Persuasion DC 15**: Detailed information about Alastair's passage
- **Persuasion DC 18**: Cryptic hints about "Night of Chains" history
- **Intimidation DC 20**: Fail—TrailBlazers respond with hostility
- **Insight DC 14**: Realize Kora knows more than she's saying

## ENVIRONMENTAL CONDITIONS

**Inside Canyon**:
- **Temperature**: -30°C (warmer than surface due to wind shelter)
- **Wind**: Minimal (canyon walls block most gusts)
- **Visibility**: Excellent (clear sightlines along canyon floor)
- **Gravity**: Stable (relatively protected from event extremes)

**Night Conditions**:
- **Lighting**: Central fire provides warmth and light (30 ft radius)
- **Cold**: Intensifies but still manageable with fire
- **Watch**: Lookouts maintain 24/7 vigilance
- **Sound**: Carries in canyon (quiet conversations echo)

**Hazards**:
- **Collapses**: Canyon walls occasionally shed rock (rare but dangerous)
- **Flash Freeze**: Underground water can freeze if temperature drops below -50°C (camp's worst fear)
- **Rustie Incursions**: Occasional scouts approach water source (TrailBlazers drive them off)

## DM NOTES

**Running the Social Encounter**:
- **First Impression**: Show humanity and culture (not just scavengers)
- **Kora's Role**: She tests party, but isn't hostile without cause
- **Information Pacing**: Give hints in Session 1, details in Session 3
- **Respect Matters**: Party's attitude shapes future relationship
- **Short Rest**: Mechanical benefit (healing, abilities) and narrative break

**Atmosphere Goals**:
- Contrast with city-states (communal vs. hierarchical)
- Show TrailBlazer strength (not victims, survivors)
- Humanize "outsiders" (they're people, not monsters)
- Plant seeds for later revelations (Night of Chains references)

**Pacing**:
- **Arrival**: 5 minutes (challenge, entry, first impressions)
- **Negotiation**: 10-15 minutes (Kora interaction, skill checks)
- **Rest/Resupply**: 5 minutes (mechanical bookkeeping, player choices)
- **Information**: 5-10 minutes (Alastair intel, cryptic warnings)
- **Departure**: 5 minutes (farewell, continue to Scar)

**Difficulty Scaling**:
- **Easy**: Kora helpful, information freely given
- **Medium**: Party must earn trust through respect/payment
- **Hard**: Party insulted TrailBlazers, must work to repair relationship

A judgment."
