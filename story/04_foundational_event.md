# 4 — THE FOUNDATIONAL EVENT

## Two Events, One Buried Truth

The history of TerraLuna is shaped by two events — one known, one taboo.

---

## Event 1: The Betrayal (Taboo, Disputed)

In the early years of the colony, a set of founders left — departing with resources that the remaining settlers believed were collectively owned. The leavers were labeled **traitors**. Their children's names were stripped from the official record so that the traitor's name would be forgotten. Instead, these children were named after their **survival pod IDs** — the alphanumeric codes of the cryo-sleep units they occupied.

For instance: **Liliane's pod was FK382 — so she is named FoxKilo.**

The children were placed in cryo-stasis while their fate was debated by the remaining founders. They stayed in stasis for **decades**.

> *What actually happened is disputed and deliberately obscured. The history as written by those who remained portrays the leavers as dangerous traitors who took resources that weren't theirs. But evidence suggests the opposite: that the 'remainers' were the aggressors — possibly violent, possibly worse — and that the leavers fled with their share of resources to survive. The 'betrayal' narrative was written by those who stayed, and it was written to protect themselves.*

This is the central buried truth of the campaign. A major revelation waits at the end of the story: the betrayal story was twisted by those who remained. The traitors may not have been traitors at all.

---

## Event 2: The Tantalos Attack (Known, Formative)

New-Rhode was attacked by Tantalos — a rival city-state — in an assault that caused massive devastation. The attack destroyed infrastructure, killed settlers, and erased institutional memory. Much of the technology the founders brought from the original expedition was lost in this attack.

After the destruction, the survivors who took over the city found the children still in cryo-stasis. They no longer knew — or no longer remembered — *why* these children were there. So they gave them names from their pod IDs and integrated them into the post-war colony as second-class citizens.

---

## The Pod-Named Citizens

These children, now adults, carry alphanumeric surnames (e.g., FoxKilo, VectorDelta, NigerSierra). They are a distinct social class. Reactions to these names vary: pity, suspicion, contempt, curiosity. There is a cultural taboo around discussing the origin of these names directly.

This is part of **Liliane FoxKilo's identity** — and part of what she addresses in her speech at the opening of the campaign, before she is shot.

---

## The Wood Problem

The TrailBlazer camp maintains a **real wood fire** — an impossibly precious resource on a metal moon with no native plant life. *Where does the wood come from?* This is a deliberate unresolved mystery for the campaign to explore.
