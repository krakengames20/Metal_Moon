# 4 - Foundational Event

## The Split

In the early colony years, the founder population fractured into two blocs.

One group — later called the Leavers — departed with resources they considered their rightful share. The departure was unannounced and perceived as a betrayal. The resources they took compounded an already fragile supply situation, and public opinion in the remaining settlement turned sharply against them.

The group that stayed — the Remainers — retained control of governance, infrastructure, and the official archive.

## The Purge

### Context

The Leavers' departure triggered a resource shortfall. The Remainer leadership faced overcapacity: too many people for the systems that remained. They lacked the infrastructure to jail dissidents or impose martial law at scale. Open violence risked destabilizing the settlement further. A political solution was required — one that could reduce population pressure, neutralize potential opposition, and consolidate authority in a single act.

### Selection

The leadership faction that would become KOR identified roughly 5% of remaining families for removal. The selection targeted the colony's least productive households — families with the weakest output metrics and the fewest institutional connections. Many also had relatives among the Leavers, which provided the necessary pretext.

The threshold of 5% was a strategic calculation. A larger purge would have provoked mass resistance. A smaller one would not have meaningfully reduced population pressure. Five percent was large enough to matter, small enough to survive public scrutiny.

### The Accusation

The selected families were accused of aiding the Leavers' escape: funneling supplies, sharing operational intelligence, and helping coordinate the departure that stripped the colony of critical resources. The charges were fabricated. Normal family contact with departed relatives was reframed as conspiracy. Circumstantial proximity became evidence of collaboration.

The Leavers were already despised for taking resources the colony could not afford to lose. Accusing the 5% of complicity channeled that anger into a verdict the majority was willing to accept.

### Sentencing

The condemned families were exiled into the desert. Their children were separated from them, declared Pupils of New-Rhode, and placed into cryostasis. Family names were stripped from the children and replaced with cryo pod identifiers. Liliane's pod was FK382; her colony name became FoxKilo.

The official framing presented cryostasis as an act of mercy — innocent children spared from the hardship of their parents' supposed treason and preserved for reintegration when the colony could support them.

### The Hostage Mechanism

The children in cryostasis functioned as hostages. The pods could be powered down from the outside but not opened. Cutting power meant suffocation, not release. As long as the children remained inside the colony, the exiled families could not organize resistance, raid supply lines, or attack New-Rhode without endangering their own children.

This deterrent did not require explicit statement. Its logic was self-evident to every exiled parent.

### Exile as Extermination

Desert exile on TerraLuna was a near-certain death sentence. Metallic dust storms, acidic weather, gravity anomalies, Rustie movement corridors, and the absence of infrastructure killed the majority of the exiled within months. Only the most resilient survivors managed to reach or establish what eventually became TrailBlazer communities.

Families who resisted the Purge vocally in the months that followed were removed through additional exile orders or through incidents recorded as operational accidents.

### The Coverup

The Purge itself is not secret. Every citizen of New-Rhode knows it happened. What KOR conceals is that the treason was fabricated — that the condemned families were innocent, that the evidence was manufactured, and that the entire operation was designed to consolidate power under the guise of justice.

KOR redacted and rewrote cryo maintenance logs and medical records from the stasis era, replacing family identifiers with pod designations. The cryo facility's sensitive sections were placed under restricted access tied to KOR-affiliated administrators. Original founder-era records were altered, though the revision logs tracking those changes were not fully purged — a latent vulnerability in the archive system.

### KOR's Founding Ethos

The faction that orchestrated the Purge consolidated over the following decades into the network now known as the Knights of Rhodes. Their governing principle across generations: the Purge was necessary for colony survival, regardless of whether the accused were guilty. The ends justified the means. Disclosure of the fabrication would delegitimize the colony's governance lineage and risk social collapse.

## The Pupils of New-Rhode

Children were thawed in staggered groups over a span of years rather than as a single cohort. Each child was raised inside the official narrative: their parents had conspired against the colony, and New-Rhode had shown mercy by saving them. Pod-ID names were framed as a clean start, free of traitorous parentage.

The social stigma attached to pod names is not an unintended side effect. It is the official narrative functioning as designed. Every distinction drawn against a pod-named citizen reinforces the premise that their families earned their condemnation.

## Present-Day Consequences

TrailBlazer communities — particularly those connected to Elder Kora Dustborn — preserve the counter-narrative through oral testimony: the families were innocent, the accusations were manufactured, the exile was a death march, and the children were taken as leverage.

Liliane FoxKilo, a descendant of the Purge and a former Pupil of New-Rhode, rose through the administrative system to become governor. She internalized the official history and became a vocal advocate for the colony's founding principles — publicly citing her own family's condemnation as evidence of New-Rhode's capacity for redemption. She does not know the treason was fabricated. Her advocacy repeats the lie from the position of its most visible victim.

Alastair, a former Deployed Admin, uncovered evidence of the fabrication through restricted archive fragments, including revision logs showing deliberate alteration of founder-era records. What radicalized him was not only the historical crime but its perpetuation in the present — specifically, that a descendant of the purged families had become the colony's most prominent voice defending the system that destroyed them.
