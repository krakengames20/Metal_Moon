# X — OPENING SCENE

## A Family During a Gravity Event

The campaign opens not with action, but with **domesticity interrupted**.

A siren sounds across New-Rhode. This sound is familiar — the gravity-event alarm. A family in their metal apartment moves through a practiced routine: children run to grab their harnesses on the wall. Parents move with calm efficiency, clipping carabiners to the ladder rungs bolted into the all-metal building's interior.

The kids are **giggling**. They've done this dozens of times. To them it's almost a game — the fun kind of scary. The kind where nothing actually bad happens.

The parents look at each other. A glance that contains: *mild worry, a shared joke, a private reassurance, a memory of the last bad one.* They look back at their children with only shallow disapproval — the disapproval of people who know that giggling is fine, actually, because this is normal now. Gravity events are survivable. The ladders work. The nets work.

Then the pull begins. Loose objects drift upward. The children grab the ladder rungs and hang there, floating slightly, laughing.

---

## Purpose of This Scene

This scene establishes:
- **Gravity events are normalized** — terrifying in concept, routine in practice
- **The ladders and nets are real infrastructure**, not decorative
- **TerraLuna families are adapted to their environment** — resilient, not helpless
- **Tone**: gritty but human, dark but not without warmth
- **The danger is real** — that parental glance holds it

The opening should feel like a world *in the middle of things* — not explaining itself to the audience, but simply living.
