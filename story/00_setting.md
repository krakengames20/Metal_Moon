# 0 — SETTING

## TerraLuna

**Metal Moon** is a tabletop RPG set in a gritty, mid-tech-maturity sci-fi world. Technology is more advanced than contemporary Earth but resources are severely unevenly distributed.

The game takes place on a moon called **TerraLuna**. It is composed mostly of hard metal, collision dust, and scattered deposits of ice — underground ice and water in disparate pockets below the surface. The surface is barren. The atmosphere is thin and hostile: cold, ionized, metallic. No Earth-bound creature can survive out in the open without protection. The "**Rusties**" — a set of hard-as-iron insect species native to TerraLuna — may be the exception. These are nothing like the insects of TerraInitia (Earth). They dissolve hard metals, except for the acid-resistant ones: Rhodium, Iridium, Tantalium, and Titanium.

The terrain is highly irregular: crater impacts, meteor strike scars, and veins of shiny cold metallic light that catch the cycles of the moon's dim illumination.

---

## The Central Mechanic: Gravity Events

The key and central concept of **Metal Moon** as a story mechanic is as follows:

TerraLuna has been drifting from its regular orbit — the orbit established when the founding colonists arrived. This drift creates a situation where the moon oscillates between increasingly intense **gravity events**. These events are mostly unpredictable. At times, gravity changes drastically.

The most common type is the **Inverted Gravity Event**: a section of the moon gets pulled by a nearby large body (a planet, a star, or something else — *we think*), causing that zone to experience reversed or wildly altered gravity.

As a result, TerraLuna's cities have built a system of **metal ladders on all buildings** and **safety nets** to prevent citizens from falling upward into the upper atmosphere during these events.

---

## The High-Activity Debris Belt

TerraLuna's metal richness comes from its interaction with a nearby **high-activity debris belt** — a dense field of asteroids and metal-rich fragments from the hard core of a star that exploded in the system. This explains why the moon's surface is so abundant with rare and precious metals.

TerraLuna also has a partial orbital relationship with a nearby **Gas Giant**, whose gravitational influence is partly responsible for the moon's orbital drift and unpredictable gravity events.
