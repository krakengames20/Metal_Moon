# 0 - Setting

## TerraLuna

Metal Moon is set on TerraLuna, a giant metal moon orbiting Typhon, a gas giant with high albedo that reflects significant sunlight onto the moon's surface.

TerraLuna is larger than Mars. Its mass is sufficient to trap a thick, breathable atmosphere. The atmosphere is dense, humid, and sulfuric, and it smells like rotten eggs.

## Atmosphere and Climate

The atmosphere is technically breathable, though colonists take medication to help with long-term respiratory health. Key environmental facts:

- **Thick humid air**: high atmospheric pressure, perpetual humidity
- **Volcanic activity**: active volcanoes and natural fusion reactors deep in the crust provide geothermal heat
- **Surface temperature**: below 10 C in most places, but above freezing
- **Acidic rain**: rainfall is chemically aggressive; large settlements use covered structures with roofs and gutters (not domes)
- **Water cycle**: liquid water exists on the surface. Vapor is abundant in the atmosphere but evaporates back into space. Water is replenished when the moon passes through Typhon's ice rings
- **Low rainfall in deserts**: most moisture stays as vapor; arid zones are dry and dusty
- **Metallic dust storms**: fine metal particulates carried by wind
- **Unstable gravity events**: localized anomalies that disrupt equipment and movement
- **Rusties**: native metal-eating lifeforms
- **Solettas**: early settlers deployed orbital mirrors (solettas) made from local metals to reflect additional sunlight and heat onto the surface
- **Space elevator**: a carbon-fibre cable system connecting the surface to orbit, used for launching solettas and orbital infrastructure

The colonies were not founded as a utopia. They were founded as a profit project.

## Transportation

Movement between colonies and remote sites relies on drones, rovers, and robotic systems. People rarely venture far from settlements due to the moon's dangers, vast scale, and limited infrastructure. Colony rules restrict unnecessary travel to preserve personnel.

## Core Premise

Each major settlement operates like a corporate city-state. Every district exists to extract, process, and ship metal. Public life, law, and security are all tied to production targets.

People survive through guilds, contracts, and ration systems. Reputation can protect you; being outside the system usually kills you.

## Buried Mystery

The public history says the colony survived because loyal founders made hard but necessary choices.

Hidden evidence suggests the official story was rewritten.

The central mystery of the campaign is not just who attacked the governor. It is who wrote colony history, who benefited from it, and who paid the real cost.

## Current Pressure Stack

The conflict is no longer only historical.

- A small high-clearance admin tier still controls restricted founder-era records
- Alastair's attack was partly driven by rage at ongoing secrecy, not only past crimes
- The legacy-family network called the **Knights of Rhodes (KOR)** is suspected of influencing political outcomes
- A newly arrived colony ship adds external tension, contract competition, and resource pressure

## Biome Unknowns

TerraLuna is still poorly understood.
- Rusties behave in coordinated patterns that do not fully match known ecology.
- Some regions show anomalies that mining science cannot yet explain.
- Founder-era survey records are incomplete or redacted.
- Volcanic zones and geothermal vents remain largely unmapped.

The moon may not be as inert as the original corporate reports claimed.
