# 2 — SOCIETY, GUILDS & CHARACTER ARCHETYPES

## Safety Tribes

Guilds on TerraLuna function as **Safety Tribes** — organizations that provide protection, income, identity, and negotiating power in a world where resources are scarce and the environment is hostile. Guild membership is not optional in any meaningful sense: independence means exposure.

The resources of TerraLuna are plentiful for industry but scarce for human life. Rich metals are everywhere; potable water is harder to reach than on Earth. There are **Founder's Wells**, rainwater collectors, and icy underground rivers that require processing before drinking.

## The Guilds

### A1. Desk Admins
Paid a minimum community income based on rank and peer feedback. Not considered a prestigious class — they are seen as organizers, low-risk bureaucrats. Liliane FoxKilo began as a Desk Admin before rising to Governor. The position is not widely respected, but it is stable. *"Not so respected position."*

### A2. Miners
A well-respected guild, sometimes called **Founderlings** — it is believed the original colony expeditions were led by Space-Miner crews. The Miners' Guild is the **only guild with the legal right to mine**. They set the price and supply of ores (comparable to OPEC), wielding enormous economic leverage. They also own and maintain the **drilling mechas** — the only machinery capable of large-scale construction or destruction. These mechs function like siege weapons when weaponized.

> *"Who do they think they are! Glorified mechanics. Without their mechs... they're nothing."* — Common complaint, said in private.

In public, miners are feared and respected.

### A3. GraviMancers
GraviMancers train to control gravity — or more accurately, to use **gravity-magnetic devices and suits** that allow them to trap, manipulate, increase, or decrease gravity in a localized area (typically a room; expert practitioners can affect a building).

They can use this to fly, to become extremely heavy, and to combine these states into devastating melee combat techniques. Examples:
- Jump high in a low-G state.
- Identify target below.
- Reverse suit to maximum heavy.
- Fall at accelerated velocity onto a pinned or defenseless target.

GraviMancers have two resource gauges:
- **Light gauge**: Fuels floating, rising, reducing personal gravity. Depleted = cannot fly.
- **Heavy gauge**: Fuels mass acceleration when falling. Depleted = cannot impact-strike.

Both gauges must be recharged independently.

### A4. Deployed Admins
Peacekeepers elected by the community (similar to sheriffs) who enforce the rules of the charter and protect citizens from harm. They also investigate violations. They are typically charismatic and prefer resolving conflict through social skills before resorting to force. Generally well-liked.

---

## TrailBlazers

An informal fifth class: desert-dwellers who live outside the city-states in the wasteland. Not a formal guild. They are descended from exiled or outcast families and have survived multiple generations in the dust desert. They possess irreplaceable knowledge of safe routes, Rustie behavior, and the locations of founder ruins and caches. They are independent, wary of city-folk, and fiercely self-reliant.
