# ELDER KORA DUSTBORN
## TrailBlazer Matriarch, Keeper of Desert Histories

**Role**: Information source, moral compass, TrailBlazer faction representative
**Guild**: TrailBlazer (Independent, Desert Tribes)
**Age**: Early 60s (appears older due to harsh environment)
**Status**: Alive, influential among desert communities

---

## PHYSICAL DESCRIPTION

**Appearance**:
- **Height**: 5'4" (163 cm), compact and weathered
- **Skin**: Deeply tanned and leathery from decades of surface exposure; network of fine wrinkles
- **Hair**: Steel-gray, long and braided with metallic beads (each bead represents a year survived)
- **Eyes**: Dark brown, sharp and knowing; seem to see through pretense
- **Notable Features**:
  - Missing tip of left pinky finger (frostbite, years ago)
  - Intricate dust-tattoos covering both forearms (TrailBlazer tribal markings)
  - Scar across right eyebrow (Rustie encounter in her youth)
  - Hands gnarled but strong
  - Walks with carved metal walking stick (practical tool and status symbol)

**Typical Attire**:
- Layered dust cloaks in earth tones (browns, grays, rust-red)
- Reinforced desert boots with magnetic grips
- Goggles pushed up on forehead (clear, not tinted—TrailBlazer tradition)
- Pouches containing survival necessities (water purification tabs, Rustie repellent, heat-tabs)
- Necklace of founder-era metal fragments (family heirlooms)

**Body Language**:
- Still and watchful (conserves energy in harsh environment)
- Direct, unblinking eye contact (assesses people quickly)
- Slow, deliberate movements (nothing wasted)
- Sits cross-legged even on uncomfortable surfaces (desert meditation practice)
- Touches earth/metal instinctively when thinking (grounds herself)

---

## PERSONALITY

**Core Traits**:
- **Wise**: Decades of desert survival and oral history keeping
- **Pragmatic**: Survival comes first, idealism second
- **Guarded**: Protects TrailBlazer secrets and interests carefully
- **Fair**: Will help outsiders who show respect and need
- **Patient**: Understands the long game; city-folk rush, desert-folk endure

**Strengths**:
- Encyclopedic knowledge of TerraLuna geography and lore
- Natural leader—TrailBlazers defer to her judgment
- Excellent judge of character (survival skill)
- Diplomatic when needed, fierce when threatened
- Keeper of oral histories city-folk have forgotten/buried

**Flaws**:
- **Grudge-Holder**: Remembers every injustice city-states inflicted on TrailBlazers
- **Cryptic**: Speaks in riddles and half-truths (tests those seeking knowledge)
- **Distrustful of City-Folk**: Default assumption is they'll betray or exploit
- **Stubborn**: Once mind is made, extremely hard to change

**Fears**:
- TrailBlazer culture being erased/forgotten
- City-states expanding into desert territories
- The truth about Night of Chains dying with her generation
- Betraying the trust of those who fled the cities 70 years ago

**Motivations**:
- Preserve TrailBlazer independence and culture
- Pass oral histories to next generation
- See the "betrayers" vindicated before she dies
- Protect desert communities from city exploitation
- Honor the memory of the first exiles

---

## BACKGROUND

**Early Life**:
- Born in the desert 60 years ago to second-generation TrailBlazers
- Grandmother was one of the original exiles (fled during Night of Chains)
- Raised on oral histories of The Lottery, The Choice, and the flight
- Trained as TrailBlazer guide from childhood
- Survived multiple near-death experiences (gravity events, Rustie swarms, dust storms)

**Rise to Elder Status**:
- Demonstrated exceptional survival skills and leadership by age 25
- Led multiple rescue missions for stranded city-folk (built reputation)
- Began formally documenting oral histories (written+memorized)
- Elected as Matriarch by TrailBlazer council at age 45
- Has maintained peace between desert tribes and city-states through strategic diplomacy

**Current Role**:
- Primary keeper of "The Night of Chains" oral history
- Mediator between TrailBlazer clans
- Occasionally deals with city representatives (pragmatic trade/information exchange)
- Mentor to younger TrailBlazers
- Guardian of sacred sites (including exile founder burial grounds)

**Connection to Conspiracy**:
- Her grandmother told her the true story of The Lottery
- Knows the "betrayers" were victims, not thieves
- Has protected this knowledge for decades (city-folk weren't ready)
- Recognizes Alastair as a truth-seeker (helped him despite risk)
- Waiting for the right moment to reveal what she knows

## DIALOGUE EXAMPLES

**First Meeting** (assessing, cautious):
> "City-folk. You hunt Alastair Redhand. We know him. He came through here two days ago. Bleeding, raving. Kept saying 'The children must know. The children must know.' We gave him water. We don't turn away the desperate. Not like the cities do."

**Cryptic Warning** (testing them):
> "Be careful what truths you dig up, city-folk. Some truths have teeth. The desert remembers what the cities forgot. Or maybe... what they chose to bury."

**Sharing Knowledge** (if trust earned):
> "My grandmother was there. The Night of Chains. She told me about the screaming—children torn from beds, parents fleeing into the dust. She told me they called US betrayers. But tell me: who betrayed whom?"

**Offering Help** (pragmatic):
> "You want to know about the founders? About what happened? I'll tell you. But not for free. The desert teaches us: everything has a cost. What will you trade for truth?"

**On City Politics** (bitter wisdom):
> "Your Governor speaks of forgiveness. Forgiveness! For what crime? Being stolen as children? Being lied to for seventy years? The cities forgive themselves while we starve in the dust. And you call US the outsiders."

**Final Wisdom** (Session 10 potential):
> "The truth will hurt. It will tear your cities apart. But lies... lies are rot. They spread. Better the quick pain of truth than the slow death of lies. My people learned that in blood and dust. Now you will too."

## KNOWLEDGE BASE

**What She Knows**:
- **The Night of Chains**: First-hand accounts from her grandmother (original exile)
- **True History**: "Betrayers" were refugees fleeing forced child removal
- **The Choice**: Founders' policy that forced exile or cryo-freezing
- **TrailBlazer Origins**: Exiled parents who fled rather than comply
- **Marcus Kaine**: Name passed down as "the architect of chains"
- **Sacred Sites**: Location of founder ruins, burial grounds, hidden caches
- **Current Politics**: City-state tensions, guild dynamics (from observation)

**What She Suspects**:
- Liliane FoxKilo doesn't know her heritage (body language, speeches)
- Some Council members are ignorant, others complicit
- The truth is close to breaking (Alastair is catalyst)
- Cities will fight to keep truth buried

**What She Doesn't Know**:
- Specific details of Marcus Kaine's confession
- Full extent of Cryo Archive tampering
- Current Council's exact knowledge levels
- Whether Liliane could handle the truth

**What She'll Share** (conditions vary):
- **Free**: Basic desert navigation help, shelter, water
- **With respect**: Hints about Night of Chains, Alastair's route
- **With trust**: Full oral history testimony
- **With alliance**: Access to sacred sites, first-generation testimonies, political support

## CULTURAL CONTEXT

**TrailBlazer Culture** (Kora represents):
- **Independence**: No guild loyalty, self-sufficient
- **Memory**: Oral histories maintained across generations
- **Honor**: Keep promises, repay debts, punish betrayals
- **Pragmatism**: Survival first, ideology second
- **Respect**: Earned through action, not status

**Night of Chains Oral History** (her responsibility to preserve):
A sung-story passed down through generations describing the night children were taken for cryo-freezing and parents fled or were executed. She can recite it word-for-word; it takes 30 minutes to perform fully.

**Sacred Sites** (under her protection):
- First-generation exile burial grounds
- Founder journal vault (hidden)
- "The Weeping Wall" (memorial to children taken)
- Underground water sources (founder wells)

## DM NOTES

**Playing Kora**:
- Speak slowly and deliberately
- Use desert/survival metaphors
- Test party's character before offering real help
- Show respect for those who show respect
- Don't give information freely—make them work for it

**Negotiation Structure**:
1. **Initial Meeting**: Assess party, offer basic help
2. **Test**: Give them a task or ask probing questions
3. **Reveal**: Share knowledge proportional to trust earned
4. **Alliance**: If fully trusted, becomes powerful recurring ally

**Information Pacing**:
- **Session 1**: Hints about Night of Chains, Alastair's direction
- **Session 3**: Full Night of Chains oral history (if players return)
- **Session 8**: Access to sacred sites and first-generation testimonies
- **Session 10**: Political support (TrailBlazers back party's choice)

**Moral Complexity**:
She's not purely good—she withholds information that could prevent suffering because she doesn't trust city-folk. Her grudges are justified but sometimes counterproductive.

---

## SECRETS & REVELATIONS

**Personal Secret**:
Her grandmother didn't just flee—she was one of the parents who tried to break into the Cryo Facility to retrieve her child. She was shot but survived. Kora knows where the child's body is buried (died in cryo when life support failed). This is deeply personal.

**Political Secret**:
She's been in contact with other alphabetic-surnamed citizens (underground network). She knows a movement is forming. Alastair wasn't working alone.

**Future Revelation** (Session 8+):
She possesses a founder journal that explicitly names Marcus Kaine as the architect and describes the executions. She's been waiting for the right moment to release it.
