# ALASTAIR REDHAND
## Former Deployed Admin, Truth-Seeker, Attempted Assassin

**Role**: Session 1 antagonist, moral grey character, potential recurring ally/enemy
**Former Guild**: Deployed Admin (resigned 6 months ago)
**Age**: Late 30s (38)
**Status**: Variable (captured/killed/escaped depending on Session 1 outcome)

---

## PHYSICAL DESCRIPTION

**Appearance**:
- **Height**: 6'1" (185 cm), lean and wiry from desert survival
- **Skin**: Weathered and sun-damaged (rare on TerraLuna; indicates extensive surface exposure)
- **Hair**: Sandy brown with premature gray streaks, unkempt, shoulder-length
- **Eyes**: Pale blue, intense and haunted; dark circles from insomnia
- **Notable Features**:
  - Bullet wound scar on right shoulder (from assassination escape)
  - Burn scars on hands (from mining equipment accident years ago)
  - Thin, sharp features (weight loss from obsession and desert living)
  - Calloused fingers stained with ink (compulsive note-taking)
  - Deployed Admin guild tattoo on forearm (tried to burn it off, left scarring)

**Typical Attire**:
- Dust-camouflage cloak (TrailBlazer style, adapted)
- Modified Deployed Admin armor (insignia removed)
- Worn crossbow (stolen Miner equipment—modified for long-range)
- Multiple pouches containing manifesto copies and evidence fragments
- Emergency breather (cracked but functional)

**Body Language**:
- Constantly scanning surroundings (paranoia from being hunted)
- Flinches at sudden movements (trauma response)
- Intense, unblinking stare when passionate
- Defensive posture even when trying to appear calm
- Tremor in hands (stress/injury)

---

## PERSONALITY

**Core Traits**:
- **Obsessive**: Consumed by uncovering the truth; sacrificed everything for it
- **Idealistic**: Believes truth must be revealed regardless of cost
- **Desperate**: Knows he's running out of time before capture/death
- **Conflicted**: Not a natural killer; assassination attempt weighs heavily on conscience
- **Righteous**: Sees himself as avenging the wronged, not committing murder

**Strengths**:
- Exceptional investigator (trained Deployed Admin skills)
- Determined and resilient
- Willing to sacrifice himself for cause
- Sees connections others miss
- Brave enough to act on convictions

**Flaws**:
- **Tunnel Vision**: So focused on exposing conspiracy he ignores collateral damage
- **Paranoia**: Trusts almost no one; sees enemies everywhere
- **Recklessness**: Assassination attempt was poorly planned (desperation move)
- **Uncompromising**: Rejects pragmatic solutions; truth or nothing
- **Guilt**: Haunted by shooting Liliane; didn't want to hurt her but saw no other way

**Fears**:
- Dying before the truth is revealed
- That he's wrong (deep down, doubts gnaw at him)
- That killing Liliane was meaningless/wrong
- Being remembered as terrorist rather than hero

**Motivations**:
- Expose The Lottery conspiracy
- Vindicate the "betrayers" (victims of the policy)
- Stop Liliane's "forgiveness" speeches (sees them as desecration)
- Avenge the frozen children and executed parents
- Give alphabetic-surnamed citizens the truth about their heritage

---

## BACKGROUND

**Early Life**:
- Born in New-Rhode to Miner family
- Showed aptitude for investigation and justice
- Joined Deployed Admin guild at age 20
- Excellent service record for 15 years (commendations, promotions)
- Known for integrity and thoroughness

**The Turning Point** (8 months ago):
- Assigned to investigate minor theft at Cryo Archive
- Stumbled upon irregularities in cryo pod activation dates
- Began investigating out of professional curiosity
- Discovered deleted records, tampered logs, buried documents
- The deeper he dug, the worse it looked
- Brought concerns to superiors—was told to drop it
- Refused; was placed on "administrative leave" (silencing attempt)

**The Descent** (6 months ago to present):
- Resigned from Deployed Admins in protest
- Went underground, continuing investigation alone
- Contacted TrailBlazers for information (oral histories)
- Found evidence of The Lottery, The Choice, Marcus Kaine's role
- Discovered Liliane FoxKilo = Liliane Kaine
- Made critical assumption: she must know and is covering it up
- Planned assassination as "only way to stop the lie"
- Failed; now hunted

**Psychological State**:
- PTSD from shooting Liliane (nightmares, flashbacks)
- Sleep-deprived and malnourished
- Borderline delusional from stress and isolation
- Simultaneously certain he's right and terrified he's wrong
- Suicidal ideation (if captured, he's failed; if truth doesn't come out, his crimes are meaningless)

## RELATIONSHIPS

**With Other NPCs**:
- **Liliane FoxKilo**: Shot her; believes she's complicit in conspiracy. Would be devastated to learn she's innocent.
- **Former Deployed Admin Colleagues**: They see him as traitor; he sees them as complicit cowards.
- **Elder Kora Dustborn**: Gave him shelter and information; one of few people he trusts.
- **Council**: Sees them as corrupt guardians of lies; they see him as dangerous radical.
- **Alphabetic-Surnamed Citizens**: He fights for them, but many see him as terrorist who hurt their hero (Liliane).

**With Player Characters**:
- **Initial**: Sees them as enemies (guild representatives hunting him)
- **Potential**: If they listen, could become desperate ally
- **Ultimate**: Depends on how they treat him—enemy, martyr, informant, or friend

## ROLE IN CAMPAIGN

**Session 1**: Primary antagonist; moral challenge (is he terrorist or truth-seeker?)

**If Captured**:
- Imprisoned; becomes informant from cell
- Provides clues and guidance
- Council pressures party to keep him quiet
- Potential ally for truth revelation
- May escape or be assassinated later

**If Killed**:
- Becomes martyr
- His manifesto spreads, inspires underground movement
- Ghost presence—his legacy haunts party decisions
- Evidence he left behind becomes breadcrumb trail

**If Escaped**:
- Recurring ally/wild card
- Provides information from shadows
- May push party toward more radical actions
- Final confrontation if party tries to bury truth

## DIALOGUE EXAMPLES

**Confrontation** (desperate, passionate):
> "You came to drag me back. Fine. Execute me. But answer this: Why does FoxKilo preach forgiveness while wearing the murderer's name? Every word she speaks is a desecration!"

**Explaining Himself** (haunted, conflicted):
> "I didn't want to hurt her. You have to believe that. But she's the face of the lie. Every speech, every 'redemption' story—it buries the truth deeper. The children deserve to know. The exiled deserve vindication. I had no choice."

**Revealing Evidence** (intense, rapid):
> "Look at this. Cryo logs—all activated the same day. Marcus Kaine's signature. The Choice policy—forced exile or freezing. Mass graves beneath Founder District. It's all there! How can you not see it?"

**If Proven Wrong About Liliane** (devastated):
> "She... she didn't know? I shot an innocent woman? No. No, she had to know. Governors have access to everything. She... oh god. What have I done?"

**Final Words** (various outcomes):
> [If dying] "The truth... it's in the cache. The Scar. Southwestern quadrant. Tell them. Tell everyone. Please..."
>
> [If captured] "Kill me if you want. The truth is already out there. You can't bury it forever."
>
> [If bargaining] "Let me go, and I'll show you everything. Every document, every witness, every crime. Help me finish this, and I'll surrender after. You have my word."

## MORAL COMPLEXITY

**Arguments For Alastair**:
- He's right about the conspiracy
- He exhausted legal channels before acting
- He was silenced and punished for seeking truth
- His assassination attempt came from desperation, not malice
- He's fighting for voiceless victims

**Arguments Against Alastair**:
- He shot an innocent woman (Liliane doesn't know the truth)
- He didn't verify his assumption about her complicity
- Violence wasn't the only option left
- His methods undermine his cause
- His obsession blinded him to collateral damage

**The Tragedy**:
Alastair is a good man driven to terrible actions by systems that punish truth-seeking. His greatest mistake wasn't being wrong about the conspiracy—it was assuming Liliane was complicit.

## DM NOTES

**Playing Alastair**:
- He's desperate, not evil
- Show his internal conflict (guilt over shooting Liliane)
- Give players opportunities to reach him through dialogue
- Don't make him a straightforward villain
- His evidence is real; his conclusions about Liliane are wrong

**Key Moments**:
- **Session 1**: Confrontation—let players choose his fate
- **Mid-Campaign** (if alive): Provides key information, shows remorse
- **Late Campaign**: Reaction when learning Liliane's innocence
- **Session 10**: Potential testimony or martyrdom

**Player Choices**:
Let players decide:
- Kill him (easier but morally questionable)
- Capture him (complex political consequences)
- Ally with him (complicity in investigation)
- Redeem him (show him Liliane's innocence)

**Redemption Arc** (if applicable):
If players convince him Liliane is innocent, he's devastated but could become powerful ally. His guilt drives him to help expose truth while trying to atone for shooting her.
