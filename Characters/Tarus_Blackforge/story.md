# TARUS BLACKFORGE
## High-Miner, Miners Guild Representative, Council Member

**Role**: Quest-giver, pragmatic faction representative, potential ally or obstacle
**Guild**: Miners (High-Miner rank—leadership position)
**Age**: Mid-50s (53)
**Status**: Alive, influential

---

## PHYSICAL DESCRIPTION

**Appearance**:
- **Height**: 5'10" (178 cm), stocky and powerfully built from decades in mining mechs
- **Skin**: Dark brown with metallic dust permanently embedded in pores (common among lifelong miners)
- **Hair**: Black with iron-gray streaks, cropped short for helmet wear
- **Eyes**: Dark brown, calculating and shrewd
- **Notable Features**:
  - Left arm is cybernetic prosthetic (mining accident 15 years ago; rhodium-reinforced)
  - Face has numerous small scars from metal fragment spray
  - Hands calloused and large, even the prosthetic shows wear patterns
  - Slight limp in right leg (old injury, refuses treatment as "badge of honor")
  - Perpetual smell of machine oil and ozone

**Typical Attire**:
- Miners Guild formal jacket (reinforced with rhodium threading—status symbol)
- Heavy work boots (magnetic soles, industrial grade)
- Tool belt even in formal settings (habit, also contains emergency equipment)
- Miners Guild insignia pin (oversized, intimidating)
- Wears prosthetic arm openly (not cosmetic—functional and visible as power statement)

**Body Language**:
- Stands with feet planted wide (mech pilot stance)
- Gestures with prosthetic arm deliberately (reminds people of guild's power)
- Direct, unwavering gaze (tests for weakness)
- Rarely smiles; when he does, it doesn't reach his eyes
- Crosses arms frequently (defensive/authoritative posture)

---

## PERSONALITY

**Core Traits**:
- **Pragmatic**: Everything is negotiation and leverage
- **Guild-Loyal**: Miners Guild interests above all else
- **Calculating**: Thinks three moves ahead politically
- **Blunt**: No patience for diplomatic niceties
- **Opportunistic**: Will exploit any situation for guild advantage

**Strengths**:
- Excellent negotiator (years of price-setting experience)
- Understands power dynamics intuitively
- Commands respect (fear?) from guild members
- Decisive leader
- Brutally honest (refreshing to some, terrifying to others)

**Flaws**:
- **Ruthless**: Will sacrifice individuals for guild/personal gain
- **Arrogant**: Believes Miners Guild is backbone of TerraLuna (he's not entirely wrong)
- **Inflexible**: Once position is set, nearly impossible to change
- **Manipulative**: Uses people as tools/assets
- **Short-Sighted**: Focuses on immediate guild power, not long-term societal health

**Fears**:
- Miners Guild losing monopoly on mining/mechs
- Resource scarcity reducing guild's leverage
- Other guilds uniting against Miners
- Physical weakness (compensates with cybernetics and authority)

**Motivations**:
- Maintain Miners Guild dominance
- Secure mining rights and mech monopoly
- Personal power and legacy
- Prove "glorified mechanics" are actually indispensable
- [[Hidden: Avoid scrutiny of guild's wartime profiteering during Tantalos conflict]]

---

## BACKGROUND

**Early Life**:
- Born into Miners Guild (third generation)
- Started as mech operator at age 16
- Lost arm in cave-in at age 38 (saved three other miners, became legend)
- Promoted to supervisor, then High-Miner by age 45
- Ruthlessly consolidated power within guild

**Political Rise**:
- Elected to Council as Miners Guild representative 8 years ago
- Forms pragmatic alliances (temporary, transactional)
- Blocked several reforms that would limit mining monopoly
- Supported Liliane's rise to Governor (saw opportunity in alphabetic-surnamed candidate)
- [[Secret: Profited heavily during Tantalos conflict by manipulating ore prices]]

**Current Position**:
- Most powerful non-Governor Council member
- Controls critical resource: mining mechs and rare metals
- Feared by other Council members (could cripple city by withholding supply)
- Maintains public image as "champion of working guild"
- Private reality: enriches self and inner circle

**Connection to Conspiracy**:
- Knows fragments of Lottery history (guild has old records)
- Doesn't care about historical truth—cares about current stability
- Views Alastair as threat to order (and therefore guild interests)
- Will support party if it serves guild; will betray them if it serves guild
- Wildcard in truth revelation—could go either way depending on advantage

## RELATIONSHIPS

**With Other NPCs**:
- **Liliane FoxKilo**: Supported her rise; sees her as useful moderate who maintains stability. Doesn't want her martyred (chaos bad for business) but also doesn't want her digging into history.
- **Alastair Redhand**: Views as dangerous radical. Wants him silenced permanently.
- **Kess Brightshield** (Chief Deployed Admin): Professional respect; occasional alliance on law-and-order issues.
- **Syl Voidwalker** (Gravimancy Matron): Rivalry—both represent powerful guilds. Mutual distrust.
- **TrailBlazers**: Contempt (sees them as failed colonists, "desert rats").

**With Player Characters**:
- **Initial**: Transactional—hires them to retrieve Alastair and stolen equipment
- **If Successful**: Rewards them, considers them useful assets
- **If Problematic**: Becomes obstacle, uses guild power to pressure them
- **Potential**: Could become patron or antagonist depending on their choices

## ROLE IN CAMPAIGN

**Session 1**: Quest-giver (Emergency Council); assigns mission to retrieve Alastair and stolen equipment

**Sessions 2-6**: Recurring authority figure:
- Provides resources if party serves guild interests
- Pressures party to stop investigation if it threatens stability
- Potential ally in navigating Council politics

**Sessions 7-10**: Faction wildcard:
- If truth threatens chaos: opposes revelation (bad for business)
- If truth can be weaponized against rivals: supports controlled disclosure
- Final position depends on how party manages him

**Key Decision Points**:
- Will he support truth revelation or suppression?
- Will he prioritize guild power or TerraLuna stability?
- Can party negotiate his support or must they work around him?

## DIALOGUE EXAMPLES

**Mission Briefing** (blunt, authoritative):
> "Alastair Redhand shot the Governor and stole guild property. Mining pressure suit, heat-tabs, scanner equipment. You bring him back—alive or dead, don't care—and you retrieve our equipment. 150 credits each. Guild pays well when we want something done right."

**On Politics** (cynical pragmatism):
> "You think I care about founder history? Ancient politics? I care about one thing: keeping the drills running. Everything else is noise. Order, stability, predictable supply chains—that's what matters. Alastair threatens that. So we fix the problem."

**Negotiating** (calculating):
> "You want guild support? Fine. Here's what that costs: you keep this investigation quiet. No public trials, no manifesto distribution, no stirring up alphabetic-surnamed citizens. We handle it internally. In exchange, I provide resources, protection, access. Deal?"

**Warning** (threatening):
> "Don't mistake pragmatism for weakness. The Miners Guild controls the mechs. The mechs control the drills. The drills control the metal. The metal controls the cities. You want to operate on TerraLuna? You operate with our permission. Remember that."

**Surprising Honesty** (rare moment of candidness):
> "You think I'm a villain. Maybe I am. But when the next resource crisis hits—and it will—my guild will keep people alive. Not speeches, not forgiveness, not historical truth. Ore and metal. That's survival. Everything else is luxury."

**If Truth Threatens Guild** (Session 10 potential):
> "You want to tear down the system? Fine. Watch what happens. Chaos. Riots. Instability. And when the dust settles, someone has to rebuild. That'll be us. The Miners. Because we're the only ones who matter. So go ahead—burn it down. We'll still be here."

## POLITICAL POSITION

**Public Stance on Key Issues**:
- **Lottery Investigation**: "Focus on present, not past. Historical grudges destabilize."
- **Liliane's Shooting**: "Terrorism. Alastair must be punished to deter future attacks."
- **TrailBlazers**: "Exiles who chose hardship over civilization. Not our problem."
- **Alphabetic-Surnamed Citizens**: "Equal rights earned through contribution. Liliane proves the system works."

**Private Beliefs**:
- Doesn't care about historical justice
- Cares about maintaining power structure that favors Miners Guild
- Willing to sacrifice truth for stability
- BUT: If chaos is inevitable, will position guild to benefit

**Leverage Points** (if party needs to manipulate him):
- **Guild Interests**: Show how their goals align with mining monopoly
- **Economic**: Demonstrate truth revelation could be managed to avoid economic disruption
- **Pride**: Appeal to his legacy (be remembered as wise leader, not tyrant)
- **Threat**: Expose guild's wartime profiteering [[requires uncovering this secret]]

## SECRETS

**Personal Secrets**:
1. **Wartime Profiteering**: During Tantalos conflict, manipulated ore prices to profit from both sides. If exposed, could lose guild position.
2. **Cybernetic Truth**: His arm wasn't destroyed in heroic rescue—it was crushed while fleeing a cave-in he caused through negligence. The "heroic rescue" story is propaganda.
3. **Political Assassination**: Was involved in removing previous High-Miner (made it look like accident). No proof, but he knows.

**Knowledge Secrets**:
1. **Founder Records**: Guild archives contain partial Lottery documentation (resource allocation logs). He's seen them but never shared.
2. **Marcus Kaine**: Knows Kaine requisitioned mining mechs for "enforcement purposes" 70 years ago. Knows what that implies.
3. **Council Knowledge**: Suspects some Council members know truth; has never pressed because it's politically inconvenient.

**Vulnerabilities**:
- Guild loyalty above all—threaten guild, he reacts
- Pride in his "heroic" story—exposing lie would devastate him
- Profiteering exposure would end his career
- Physical: prosthetic can be EMP-disabled (2d6 Constitution save or paralyzed arm for 1 minute)

## DM NOTES

**Playing Tarus**:
- Speak in short, blunt sentences
- Make everything transactional
- Show physical power (gestures with prosthetic)
- Never apologize or show weakness
- Use economic/guild threats casually

**Party Dynamics**:
- **Miner PC**: He expects loyalty; betrayal would be personal
- **Other PCs**: Sees as tools/assets; respect only if they prove useful
- **If Opposed**: Becomes dangerous enemy (guild resources)
- **If Allied**: Powerful but unreliable ally (will betray if convenient)

**Character Arc Potential**:
- **Redemption**: Learns guild power isn't everything; supports truth for legacy
- **Villain**: Doubles down, becomes final obstacle to truth revelation
- **Pragmatist**: Accepts truth but manages it for minimum disruption
- **Downfall**: Wartime profiteering exposed; loses everything

**Information He Can Provide** (if leveraged):
- Guild archives with partial Lottery records
- Mech requisition logs from Marcus Kaine era
- Political intelligence on other Council members
- Resources (equipment, safe houses, credits)
